Who is Jimmy Butler?

Jimmy Butler is an American basketball player who plays as a Shooting guard and Small forward for the Chicago Bulls of the National Basketball Association (NBA).

Before fame, he played college basketball at Tyler Junior College and Marquette University.

He was named the NBA’s Most Improved Player of 2015.

Jimmy Butler: Birth Facts, Family, Childhood

Jimmy was born Jimmy Butler III on September 14, 1989, in Houston, Texas, United States. He is of African-American ethnicity.

His mother is Londa Butler. He has a brother named Jordan Leslie who played football at the University of Texas, El Paso.

His father abandoned his mother, brother, and him when he was a child. His mother kicked him out of the house when he was 13 years old.

In which school did Jimmy Butler attend?

Jimmy Butler attended Tomball High School and later enrolled at Tyler Junior College from where he graduated in 2008.

After college graduation, he attended Marquette University in 2008 and graduated in 2011.

He played as a Shooting guard in his high school and the university team.

During his college, Jimmy Butler was voted his team’s most valuable player.

Jimmy Butler: Professional Life, Career

Butler began his basketball career playing for his college and university team. During his days at Marquette University, he gained interest from Division I programs and he was considered a two-star recruit by 247Sports.com.

He was listed as the No. 127 junior college prospect in 2008.  Chicago Bulls signed him in the 2011 NBA draft. In July 2012, Butler joined the Bulls for the 2012 NBA Summer League and shortly, he became successful to gain a positive response from the board members. Jimmy was named to the NBA All-Defensive second team in June 2014.

In December 2014, Jimmy Butler was named the Eastern Conference Player of the Month. He re-signed with the Bulls to a five-year, $95 million contract in July 2015. He was rewarded with his first career All-Star nod as well as the NBA’s Most Improved Player Award in the 2014-15 season.
