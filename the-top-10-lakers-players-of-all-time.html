The Top 10 Lakers Players of All Time

July 25, 2024
blog

The Los Angeles Lakers have a storied history in the NBA, boasting numerous championship titles and a roster of legendary players. From the early years in Minneapolis to the glitz and glamour of Hollywood, the Lakers have been home to some of the greatest basketball players of all time. In this article, we will explore the top 10 Lakers players of all time, their achievements, and their impact on the franchise.

1. Magic Johnson

Magic Johnson is often hailed as the greatest Laker of all time. He played his entire career with the Lakers and led the team to five NBA championships. Known for his exceptional passing skills and court vision, Johnson revolutionized the point guard position. He was a 12-time All-Star and won three NBA MVP awards during his career.

2. Kobe Bryant

Kobe Bryant is undoubtedly one of the most iconic Lakers players in history. With five NBA championships to his name, Bryant was a fierce competitor and a scoring machine. He spent his entire 20-year career with the Lakers and became the franchise's all-time leading scorer. Bryant's dedication to the game and his Mamba Mentality made him a fan favorite and a true Lakers legend.

3. Kareem Abdul-Jabbar

Kareem Abdul-Jabbar is widely regarded as one of the greatest basketball players of all time. He joined the Lakers in 1975 after winning a championship with the Milwaukee Bucks. Abdul-Jabbar dominated the game with his unstoppable skyhook shot and incredible scoring ability. He won five championships with the Lakers and is the NBA's all-time leading scorer.

4. Shaquille O'Neal

Shaquille O'Neal, also known as Shaq, was a force to be reckoned with during his time with the Lakers. He formed a deadly partnership with Kobe Bryant and together they won three consecutive NBA championships from 2000 to 2002. O'Neal's size, strength, and dominance in the paint made him one of the most unstoppable players of his era.

5. Jerry West

Jerry West, also known as "Mr. Clutch," is the only player on this list who did not win an NBA championship with the Lakers. However, his impact on the franchise cannot be overlooked. West was a 14-time All-Star and is the Lakers' all-time leading scorer. His iconic silhouette serves as the NBA logo, a testament to his greatness.

6. Elgin Baylor

Elgin Baylor was a pioneer of the game and one of the most athletic players of his time. He played his entire career with the Lakers and was known for his acrobatic moves and scoring ability. Despite never winning an NBA championship, Baylor's impact on the game and the Lakers franchise is undeniable.

7. Wilt Chamberlain

Wilt Chamberlain is considered one of the most dominant players in NBA history. Although he played for multiple teams throughout his career, his time with the Lakers was memorable. Chamberlain won the NBA championship with the Lakers in 1972 and set numerous records along the way.

8. James Worthy

James Worthy, also known as "Big Game James," was an integral part of the Lakers' "Showtime" era in the 1980s. He played alongside Magic Johnson and Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, helping the team win three NBA championships. Worthy's ability to perform in clutch moments earned him his nickname and a place among the top Lakers players of all time.

9. George Mikan

George Mikan was a pioneer of the game and one of the first dominant big men in basketball. He led the Lakers to five championships in the NBA's early years and was known for his scoring and rebounding ability. Mikan's impact on the game laid the foundation for future generations of players.

10. Jerry Buss (Owner)

While not a player, Jerry Buss deserves recognition for his role in shaping the Lakers' success. Buss purchased the team in 1979 and transformed it into one of the most successful franchises in NBA history. Under his ownership, the Lakers won ten championships and became synonymous with excellence.

The Los Angeles Lakers have been home to some of the greatest basketball players of all time. From Magic Johnson to Kobe Bryant, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar to Shaquille O'Neal, each player on this list has left an indelible mark on the franchise and the game. These players' skill, dedication, and achievements have cemented their place among the top Lakers players of all time.
