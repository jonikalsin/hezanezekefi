Terrance Williams: An Exciting Journey Through Football

August 05, 2024

Terrance Tyrone Williams, born on September 8, 1989, is an American football wide receiver currently playing for the Galgos de Tijuana of the Liga de Fútbol Americano Profesional (LFA).

Together with Fan Arch, let's delve into the in-depth analysis of his career, starting from his early life and progressing through his professional journey in the NFL and beyond.


Early Life and College Career

Williams was born in Highland Hills, Dallas, and played high school football at W. T. White High School where he was a standout wide receiver.

He transferred to Baylor University, where he played for the Baylor Bears football team from 2008 to 2012.

 He most certainly showcased his talents during his college career - catching 202 passes for 3,334 yards and 27 touchdowns. He led the nation with 1,832 receiving yards in 2012 and unanimously became an All-American that season.


Professional Career

Williams opened his journey into the NFL when he was selected by the Dallas Cowboys in the 3rd round of the 2013 NFL Draft.

As a rookie, he was a large part of the offense, and finished with 44 receptions, for 736 yards and five touchdowns. In subsequent years, he showed promise and solidified his spot as the clear No. 2 wide receiver and deep threat across from Dez Bryant.

He had his ups and downs, but displayed his skills with a career game statistically in the regular-season finale against the Washington Redskins, making eight receptions for 173 yards.
