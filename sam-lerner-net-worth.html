Sam Lerner Biography

He is an American actor, best known for his role as Geoff Schwartz in The Goldbergs, Chowder in Monster House, and Quinn Goldberg in Project Almanac. Sam is a social media personality for Sweetgreen. Lerner starred in Envy's feature film as the son of Ben Stiller and Rachel Weisz's characters and played the son of Wendie Malick and Sam Robard's characters in ABC's pilot, My Life With Men, and the son of John Leguizamo and Claire Forlani's characters in CBS-TV's untitled Brett Ratner's pilot. Lerner was one of the directors, Chowder, in the computer-animated film Monster House (2006). He was nominated for the 2006 Annie Award for Best Voice Acting in Animated Feature for his role and was awarded the award by Sir Ian McKellen. He appeared on the television shows Malcolm in the Middle, The King of Queens, Two and a Half Guys, Oliver Beene, and Sonny With a Chance, and played a recurring voice on the Cartoon Network show What Happened to Robot Jones? He did vocal work for Zak Saturday, the character of the Cartoon Network show, The Secret Saturdays. On October 16, 2017, Lerner was seen as Ronnie in the Blumhouse supernatural thriller film Truth or Dare. The film was released in theatres on 13 April 2018.
